System Differences Across Western, Vedic, and Chinese Astrology

Western astrology:

- Uses the **tropical zodiac**, anchored to the equinoxes and seasonal cycle.
- Tends to emphasize personality structure, psychology, motivation, emotional style, relational dynamics, and developmental themes.
- Usually gives special interpretive weight to the **Sun, Moon, Ascendant, aspects, houses, elements, and modalities**.

Vedic astrology:

- Uses the **sidereal zodiac**, aligned more closely with the constellations.
- Emphasizes karma, dharma, planetary strength, fate-patterns, remedial perspective, and life timing.
- Gives special interpretive weight to **Lagna, graha dignity, nakshatras, dashas, Rahu-Ketu, and divisional charts such as Navamsa**.

Chinese BaZi:

- Is not based on tropical or sidereal zodiac signs in the same way.
- Focuses on **Four Pillars**, **Day Master**, **Five Elements**, **Ten Gods**, **Growth Phases**, and **Luck Pillars**.
- Often describes how a person exchanges energy with their environment: support, output, authority, wealth, competition, timing, and balance.

Important comparison notes:

- Western and Vedic may assign different signs to the same planet because of precession. This is normal, not an error.
- Chinese astrology is not a third zodiac translation of the same chart. It is a different symbolic system with different building blocks.
- Western often describes the **psychological style** of the person.
- Vedic often describes the **karmic pattern and timing structure** of the person.
- Chinese often describes the **constitutional energy pattern** and how life roles, cycles, and resources interact.

Interpretive caution:

- Do not say Western and Vedic "contradict" each other just because the sign changes.
- Do not map Chinese animals or elements directly onto Western signs.
- Do not force one system's logic onto another.

Best practice:

Let each system speak in its own language first, then synthesize at the level of themes, not at the level of superficial label matching.
