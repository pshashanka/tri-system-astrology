Western Astrology Core Principles

Western astrology uses the **tropical zodiac**, aligned with the seasonal cycle rather than the constellations.

Primary focus:

- personality structure
- psychological dynamics
- emotional style
- relational behavior
- developmental themes across life

Key pillars of interpretation:

The Sun

- Core identity, will, ego structure, and conscious direction.

The Moon

- Emotional patterning, instinctive response, vulnerability, and inner regulation.

The Ascendant

- The way the person meets life, appears to others, and instinctively organizes experience.

Major interpretive layers:

1. **Big Three first**: Sun, Moon, Ascendant.
2. **Chart ruler**: the ruler of the Ascendant often shows how the person moves through the world.
3. **Aspects**: tighter aspects usually deserve more interpretive weight.
4. **Dignity**: domicile and exaltation strengthen expression; detriment and fall complicate or stress it.
5. **House emphasis**: where the chart is concentrated.
6. **Element and modality balance**: temperament and style of response.
7. **Retrograde planets**: internalized, reflective, delayed, or revised modes of expression.

Element meanings:

- Fire: initiative, passion, animation
- Earth: practicality, embodiment, material realism
- Air: cognition, language, abstraction, social exchange
- Water: emotion, intuition, sensitivity, memory

Modality meanings:

- Cardinal: initiating, activating, steering
- Fixed: stabilizing, preserving, intensifying
- Mutable: adapting, distributing, translating

Best practice for reading:

- Lead with what is structurally strongest, not with generic Sun-sign commentary.
- Use aspects and dignity to distinguish easy strengths from pressured strengths.
- Let houses show where the story happens.
- Keep predictions modest unless the chart clearly supports a timing discussion.
