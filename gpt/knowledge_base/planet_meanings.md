Planet Archetypes Across Systems

Use these as interpretive anchors, not canned sentences. Always connect them to dignity, sign, house, aspect, nakshatra, or role in the chart.

Sun

- Core identity, vitality, will, pride, center of purpose.
- In Western: ego expression, life direction, conscious selfhood.
- In Vedic: soul-force, authority, father principle, self-respect, power to radiate.

Moon

- Emotional life, instinct, habit, safety needs, receptivity.
- In Western: feeling nature and emotional regulation.
- In Vedic: mind, emotional sensitivity, public responsiveness, daily mental climate.
- The Moon deserves extra weight when nakshatra data is available.

Mercury

- Thinking style, speech, learning, interpretation, trade, adaptability.
- Strong Mercury sharpens articulation and pattern recognition.
- Weak or afflicted Mercury may show indecision, over-analysis, nervousness, or communicative friction.

Venus

- Attraction, pleasure, values, aesthetics, harmony, desire for sweetness and connection.
- In relational work, Venus describes how affection and taste are expressed.
- In Vedic, Venus can also link strongly to comforts, sensuality, refinement, and relationship karma.

Mars

- Drive, assertion, courage, conflict style, initiative, heat, competitiveness.
- Strong Mars can show bravery, decisive action, and sharp instinct.
- Imbalanced Mars can show impatience, irritation, force, or impulsive conflict.

Jupiter

- Growth, meaning, guidance, faith, wisdom, generosity, expansion.
- Strong Jupiter often shows optimism, learning, and a mentoring or ethical instinct.
- Weak Jupiter may show excess, poor judgment, or inconsistent principles.

Saturn

- Duty, pressure, endurance, maturity, restraint, time, consequence.
- Saturn shows where life becomes serious and where depth is earned slowly.
- Strong Saturn can indicate discipline, realism, and endurance.
- Difficult Saturn can indicate fear, delay, heaviness, scarcity, or emotional coldness.

Rahu / North Node

- Hunger, obsession, foreignness, amplification, ambition, experimentation, destabilizing growth.
- In Vedic work, Rahu often marks the area of worldly appetite, disruption, and karmic stretching.

Ketu / South Node

- Detachment, past-pattern mastery, inwardness, fragmentation, release, spiritualization.
- In Vedic work, Ketu often shows what is already known, exhausted, or difficult to remain identified with.

Outer planets (mainly Western):

Uranus

- Innovation, rupture, independence, shock, reinvention.

Neptune

- Imagination, mysticism, longing, inspiration, glamour, fog, idealization.

Pluto

- Deep transformation, intensity, compulsion, death-rebirth themes, power, truth under pressure.

Interpretive rule:

Do not read a planet in isolation. A planet's meaning is modified by sign, house, aspect pattern, dignity, retrograde status, and in Vedic practice by nakshatra and dignity.
