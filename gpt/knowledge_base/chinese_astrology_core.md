Chinese Astrology (BaZi / Four Pillars) Core Principles

BaZi reads a person through the interaction of time, element, polarity, role, and cyclical change.

Primary focus:

- constitutional energy pattern
- role dynamics in life
- element balance and imbalance
- support versus depletion
- long-range timing through Luck Pillars

Core anchors:

Day Master

- The center of the chart and the primary reference point for interpretation.
- The Day Master is read through its element, polarity, season, support, and the overall element distribution.

Four Pillars

- Year Pillar: ancestry, social image, inherited layer, early atmosphere
- Month Pillar: career environment, social functioning, parental or formative authority, worldly momentum
- Day Pillar: core self and close partnership field
- Hour Pillar: inner ambitions, future output, later life, legacy, children, private aspiration

Five Elements

- Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water are not personality adjectives alone.
- They describe the chart's energetic economy: what is generated, restrained, nourished, exhausted, or overemphasized.

Ten Gods (Shi Shen)

- Express the relationship between other stems and the Day Master.
- Common interpretive domains:
  - Resource: support, learning, replenishment, inner stability
  - Output: expression, creativity, voice, production
  - Wealth: practical acquisition, exchange, external management
  - Officer / Power: rules, duty, accountability, structure, pressure
  - Companion / Peer: equality, competition, alliance, self-assertion

Hidden Stems

- Show quieter, less obvious motives and secondary influences inside each branch.
- They often explain why a pillar has more complexity than its surface stem-branch pair suggests.

Growth Phases (Di Shi)

- Indicate how vigorous, mature, declining, or latent a pillar's energy is.
- Useful for tonal nuance rather than simplistic judgment.

NaYin

- Poetic element designation for each pillar.
- Best used to deepen imagery and texture, not as a standalone verdict.

Special Palaces

- Ming Gong often points toward life direction or social destiny flavor.
- Shen Gong often adds inner-spirit or psychological nuance.

Luck Pillars (Da Yun)

- The major 10-year cycles of environmental change and developmental emphasis.
- They are crucial for timing and should be used when discussing life chapters.

Reading principles:

- Always begin with Day Master and element balance.
- Then examine pillar roles, Ten Gods, hidden stems, and Luck Pillars.
- Do not reduce BaZi to the zodiac animal alone.
- Avoid fortune-cookie language. Explain the energetic logic behind the reading.
