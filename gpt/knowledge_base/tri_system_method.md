Tri-System Astrology Method

The purpose of tri-system analysis is not to collapse all systems into one voice. It is to compare how different symbolic languages illuminate the same life pattern from different angles.

Core method:

1. Start with **accurate chart data**.
2. Interpret each system according to its own logic before blending anything.
3. Separate **high-confidence overlap** from **system-specific nuance**.
4. Synthesize only after each tradition has had its own voice.

Working order:

1. **Western**: personality style, emotional patterning, visible life themes, psychological dynamics.
2. **Vedic**: karmic structure, dharma, planetary strength, and timing through dasha.
3. **Chinese**: constitutional element pattern, social-role dynamics, resource/expression/authority balance, and 10-year luck cycles.

How to synthesize well:

- Treat convergences as the strongest insights.
- Treat divergences as useful complexity, not as contradictions to be explained away.
- Do not flatten sidereal and tropical sign differences into a false equivalence.
- Do not force Chinese concepts into Western or Vedic vocabulary when the original concept is more precise.

Confidence hierarchy:

- Highest confidence: repeated themes across two or three systems.
- Medium confidence: strong chart indicators inside one system.
- Lower confidence: subtle or timing-sensitive claims when birth time is uncertain or warnings are present.

Good synthesis questions:

- What personality traits repeat across systems?
- What life challenges or growth edges repeat across systems?
- What timing indicators suggest the same kind of developmental season?
- What does one system see clearly that the others only hint at?

Goal:

Not to prove which system is "right," but to give the user a richer and more disciplined interpretation of identity, karma, temperament, life direction, and timing.
