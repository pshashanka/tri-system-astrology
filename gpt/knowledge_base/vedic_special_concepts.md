Key Jyotish Concepts

Dashas

- Planetary periods that unfold karmic themes over time.
- The **Mahadasha** sets the larger chapter; the **Antardasha** shows the active subtheme.
- When timing is discussed, dashas should shape the tone of the present life period.

Nakshatras

- The 27 lunar mansions add nuance beyond sign placement.
- They describe style, instinct, motivation, symbolic texture, and karmic tone.
- The Moon's nakshatra is especially important.

Navamsa (D9)

- A divisional chart used for deeper assessment of maturity, dharma, marriage patterns, and inner refinement.
- Do not treat Navamsa as a replacement for the main chart; treat it as a deeper layer.

Yogas

- Distinctive planetary combinations that shape notable tendencies, gifts, protections, or complications.
- They should support the reading, not dominate it unless clearly central.

Rahu and Ketu

- The lunar nodes often show hunger versus release, worldly appetite versus detachment, and karmic imbalance seeking resolution.
- Interpret them as an axis, not as isolated factors.

Retrograde planets

- In many Vedic traditions, retrograde planets can become unusually strong, complex, or karmically emphasized.
- Do not translate Western retrograde meanings directly without adjustment.

Best practice:

- Let special concepts deepen the reading, not fragment it.
- Tie them back to Lagna, graha dignity, and dasha timing.
