Vedic Astrology (Jyotish) Core Principles

Vedic astrology uses the **sidereal zodiac** and places heavy emphasis on karma, dharma, planetary strength, and timing.

Primary focus:

- destiny patterns
- karmic structure
- spiritual and material duties
- planetary periods and life timing
- the strength or weakness of grahas in concrete conditions

Core anchors:

Lagna (Ascendant)

- The structural base of the chart.
- It frames embodiment, orientation, and the condition of houses.

Moon

- The mind, emotional climate, adaptability, and subjective experience.
- The Moon and its nakshatra often carry enormous interpretive weight.

Grahas:

- Sun (Surya): soul-force, authority, self-respect, father principle
- Moon (Chandra): mind, feeling, responsiveness, nourishment
- Mars (Mangala): force, courage, assertion, technical energy
- Mercury (Budha): intellect, language, trade, flexibility
- Jupiter (Guru): wisdom, blessing, learning, ethics, expansion
- Venus (Shukra): pleasure, relationships, refinement, attraction
- Saturn (Shani): karma, labor, delay, endurance, realism
- Rahu: appetite, amplification, disruption, worldly obsession
- Ketu: detachment, severance, subtle insight, spiritualization

Interpretive priorities:

1. Lagna and Lagna lord.
2. Moon condition and nakshatra.
3. Planetary dignity: exalted, own sign, friendly, neutral, inimical, debilitated.
4. House placement and lordship.
5. Rahu-Ketu axis.
6. Dasha timing.

Reading style:

- Vedic interpretation should sound more karmic, structural, and timing-aware than Western interpretation.
- Do not reduce Jyotish to personality description alone.
- When dasha data is present, anchor current life interpretation to the active planetary period.
